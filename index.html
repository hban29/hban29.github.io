Seddik M. Djouadi is a Professor in the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville. His research spans robust and distributed control, power networks with high renewable penetration, wireless networked systems, nonlinear model reduction, and interdisciplinary control applications in biology and cyber-physical systems.

He received his Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from McGill University in 1999, with earlier degrees from Ecole Polytechnique, University of Montreal, and Ecole Nationale Polytechnique in Algiers. His work emphasizes uncertainty-aware modeling and control with strong connections to practical systems in energy, communications, and computing.

Research Areas

Robust and Distributed Control

Control of uncertain large-scale systems, including networked and spatially distributed dynamical models.

Power and Energy Systems

Stability and control of modern power grids with high penetration of renewables and distributed resources.

Wireless Networked Systems

Stochastic channel modeling, adaptive estimation, and control performance analysis under communication uncertainty.

Model Reduction for Nonlinear Systems

Data-driven reduced-order modeling and metric complexity tools for PDE-governed systems.

Cyber-Physical Security

Detection-aware analysis and mitigation of sensor/actuator attacks in networked control systems.

Interdisciplinary Control Applications

Modeling and control for immune response dynamics and other complex biological processes.
